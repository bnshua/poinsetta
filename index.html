Poinsettia

Pet Safety Guide: what happens if your cat or dog eats poinsettia, what to do, and how to prevent it.

Clinical Signs of Ingestion

Visible symptoms after eating poinsettias.

  • Mild mouth or stomach irritation
  • Drooling or pawing at the mouth
  • Vomiting
  • Occasional diarrhea
  • Decreased appetite or mild lethargy

Most reactions are mild, but any worsening symptoms matter.

Treatment

What to do if your animal eats part of the plant

  1. Remove any plant pieces from your pet’s mouth.
  2. Offer a small amount of water to rinse/soothe irritation.
  3. Monitor for vomiting, diarrhea, or unusual behavior for 24 hours.
  4. Call your vet if symptoms are persistent, severe, or if your pet has existing health issues.
  5. If your pet ate a large amount or seems distressed, seek veterinary care immediately.
Emergency tip: Bring a photo or a piece of the plant to the vet so they can identify it quickly.

Prevention

How to prevent this from happening

  • Place poinsettias on high shelves or in rooms pets can’t access.
  • Use hanging planters or closed terrariums if you want them visible.
  • Clean fallen leaves immediately so curious pets don’t snack.
  • Consider pet-safe holiday plants as alternatives.
  • Teach “leave it” and reward pets for ignoring plants.
