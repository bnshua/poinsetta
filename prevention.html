Prevention

How to prevent this from happening

Poinsettias usually cause mild problems, but avoiding the problem is still easier than cleaning vomit at 2am while questioning every life choice you ever made.

1. Put Plants Out of Reach

If they can’t get to it, they can’t eat it.

  • Use high shelves or hanging planters.
  • Keep plants in rooms pets don’t enter.
  • Avoid leaving pots near couches or climbable furniture.

2. Block Access

Physical barriers beat wishful thinking.

  • Use baby gates or closed doors for plant areas.
  • Try a decorative fence around floor plants.
  • Place pots inside heavier containers pets can’t tip.

3. Clean Fallen Leaves Fast

Pets love mystery snacks from the floor.

  • Pick up dropped leaves right away.
  • Vacuum around the plant often.
  • Check under tables where pieces might fall unnoticed.

4. Train “Leave It”

A little training goes a long way.

  • Reward them for ignoring plants.
  • Practice with treats, slowly increasing distraction.
  • Be consistent, even when you’re exhausted.

It’s annoying, but it works. unlike most things.

5. Give Them Better Options

If they’re bored, plants become entertainment.

  • Provide chew toys (dogs) or cat grass (cats).
  • Rotate toys so they stay interesting.
  • Use puzzle feeders to keep them busy.

6. Choose Pet-Safe Plants

Sometimes it’s easier to swap the plant.

  • Look for non-toxic holiday plants as alternatives.
  • Ask your vet or check ASPCA plant safety lists.
  • When unsure, assume it’s not safe until confirmed.
  • Note, this is a last option security measure. Do it if no other options are available.
