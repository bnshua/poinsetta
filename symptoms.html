Clinical Signs of Ingestion

Visible symptoms of pets after eating poinsettias

Poinsettias are low-toxicity for cats and dogs, so most reactions stay mild. Still, your pet’s discomfort is real, and you should watch for changes.

1. Most Common Mild Signs

These show up most often, especially with small bites.

  • Mouth irritation (licking lips, pawing at mouth)
  • Drooling or foamy saliva
  • Mild vomiting (usually 1–2 times)
  • Soft stool or a little diarrhea
  • Temporary lower appetite

Most pets bounce back within a few hours to a day.

2. Digestive Reactions

If they swallowed plant pieces or sap, the stomach might complain.

  • Stomach irritation causing nausea
  • Vomiting soon after chewing the plant
  • Occasional diarrhea
  • Gagging if bits briefly stick in the throat
Usually mild. If it keeps happening or worsens, that’s not “normal.”

3. Behavior Changes

Sometimes irritation makes them act “off.”

  • Mild lethargy (sleepier than usual)
  • Restlessness or discomfort
  • Face rubbing on carpet/furniture
  • Whining or clingy behavior

4. Sap Irritation (Skin / Eyes)

The white sap can be annoying if it gets on them.

  • Watery or squinty eyes if they rubbed sap into their face
  • Redness around mouth from direct contact
  • Face/ear scratching because it feels itchy
  • Mild skin redness where sap touched fur

The sap is more irritating than dangerous.

5. Typical Timeline

When symptoms usually show up.

  • 0–2 hours: drooling, pawing at mouth, lip smacking
  • 2–6 hours: mild vomiting or soft stool
  • 6–24 hours: appetite dip or tiredness, then improvement
If symptoms are worse after 24 hours, call a vet.

6. Red Flags (Call Your Vet)

Not common, but take these seriously.

  • Vomiting or diarrhea lasting past ~24 hours
  • Repeated vomiting (more than 2–3 times)
  • Refusing food + water completely
  • Swelling around mouth/face
  • Breathing trouble or extreme weakness
  • Your pet is very young, old, or has health issues
If you’re unsure, calling is always safer than waiting.
